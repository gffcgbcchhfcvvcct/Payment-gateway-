Payment Processing

5.1 How SwiftPay Processes Payments

SwiftPay acts as a payment intermediary, facilitating the transfer of funds from a Payer's mobile money wallet to the Merchant's SwiftPay holding account, pending settlement. SwiftPay does not hold Merchant funds indefinitely; all collected funds are disbursed in accordance with the settlement schedule outlined in Section 7.

5.2 Supported Countries & Mobile Money Networks

SwiftPay currently supports payment processing in the following countries and through the listed mobile money networks:

Country Currency Supported Networks
🇿🇲 Zambia ZMW — Zambian Kwacha MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, Zamtel Kwacha
🇧🇼 Botswana BWP — Botswana Pula Orange Money, MyZaka, BTC Mobile
🇿🇼 Zimbabwe ZWL / USD — Zimbabwean Dollar / US Dollar EcoCash, OneMoney, Telecash
🇳🇦 Namibia NAD — Namibian Dollar MTC MobiPay, TN Mobile Money, Telecom Namibia Pay

SwiftPay reserves the right to add or remove supported networks at any time. Merchants will be notified of any changes affecting networks they are actively using.

5.3 Transaction Currency

All transactions must be denominated in a Supported Currency corresponding to the country in which the payment is being processed. Cross-currency transactions are not supported unless explicitly enabled for a specific Merchant account. For Zimbabwe, both ZWL and USD are accepted, with currency selection determined by the Merchant at the time of transaction initiation.

5.4 Transaction Limits

Individual transaction amounts are subject to the minimum and maximum limits imposed by each mobile money network operator. SwiftPay may additionally apply its own transaction limits based on a Merchant's account verification level. Merchants requiring higher limits should contact elicatetechnologies@gmail.com to discuss enterprise arrangements.

5.5 Transaction Authorisation

A transaction is only considered successful and authorised when SwiftPay receives a confirmed settlement confirmation from the relevant mobile money network. Pending transactions are not guaranteed to complete. Merchants must not fulfil orders or deliver services until a transaction status of SUCCESS is received via the API response or Webhook notification.

5.6 Failed Transactions

SwiftPay is not liable for transactions that fail due to insufficient funds in the Payer's wallet, network downtime on the part of mobile money operators, incorrect phone numbers, or any other reason beyond SwiftPay's reasonable control. Failed transactions are not subject to SwiftPay's processing fee.

5.7 Reversals

Where a transaction is erroneously processed due to a technical error on the SwiftPay platform, SwiftPay will initiate a reversal or refund in accordance with Section 8. Merchant-initiated reversals must be requested within 30 days of the original transaction date.

Fees & Charges

6.1 Processing Fee

SwiftPay charges a flat processing fee of 2.6% per successful transaction. This fee is automatically deducted from the transaction amount before settlement. There are no monthly subscription fees, setup fees, or minimum usage fees.

6.2 Fee Structure Summary

  • Transaction Fee: 2.6% of successful transaction value
  • Monthly Base Fee: None
  • Setup Fee: None

All fees are deducted at source, meaning the fee is subtracted from the gross transaction amount before the net amount is credited to the Merchant's SwiftPay holding account. For example, a ZMW 100.00 transaction will result in a net credit of ZMW 97.40 to the Merchant's account (ZMW 2.60 fee at 2.6%).

Payouts & Settlements

7.1 Settlement Schedule

SwiftPay processes Merchant settlements on a T+1 basis (the next Business Day after transaction confirmation), subject to the following conditions:

  • The Merchant's account has successfully completed KYC verification.
  • The Merchant has a valid and verified settlement destination (bank account or mobile money wallet).
  • No holds, investigations, or account restrictions are active on the Merchant's account.
  • The total pending settlement balance exceeds the applicable minimum settlement threshold.

7.2 Minimum Settlement Threshold

The minimum settlement amount per payout cycle is as follows:

  • 🇿🇲 Zambia: ZMW 50.00
  • 🇧🇼 Botswana: BWP 20.00
  • 🇿🇼 Zimbabwe: USD 5.00 or ZWL equivalent
  • 🇳🇦 Namibia: NAD 50.00

Balances below the minimum threshold will roll over to the next settlement cycle.

7.3 Settlement Destination

Merchants may configure their settlement to be paid to a registered bank account or a verified mobile money wallet. Settlement destinations must be in the same country and currency as the transactions being settled. Cross-border settlements are not supported without a prior written arrangement with SwiftPay.

7.4 Settlement Holds

SwiftPay may place a temporary settlement hold on a Merchant's account in the following circumstances:

  • Suspected fraudulent activity or unusual transaction patterns
  • Receipt of a formal dispute, chargeback, or reversal request
  • Regulatory investigation or law enforcement request
  • Failure to comply with KYC requirements
  • Breach of these Terms of Service

SwiftPay will notify the Merchant of any settlement hold and the reason for it within 2 Business Days, unless prohibited by law. Settlement holds may remain in place until the underlying issue is resolved.

7.5 Settlement Errors

In the event of an erroneous settlement (overpayment or underpayment), the Merchant must notify SwiftPay within 5 Business Days of receiving the settlement notification. SwiftPay will investigate and correct any verified errors. Any overpayments received by the Merchant must be returned within 7 Business Days upon request.

SwiftPay is a payment intermediary and does not manage disputes between Merchants and their customers (Payers). Merchants are solely responsible for establishing, communicating, and honouring a clear and fair refund policy for their customers. SwiftPay will only process refunds upon a formal, verified request from the Merchant.

8.2 Initiating a Refund

To initiate a refund for a completed transaction, the Merchant must:

  • Submit a refund request via the SwiftPay Dashboard or via the Refund API endpoint within 30 days of the original transaction date.
  • Provide the original Transaction ID, the amount to be refunded (partial or full), and the reason for the refund.
  • Ensure sufficient balance exists in their SwiftPay holding account to cover the refund amount.

Refunds are subject to a processing time of 1–3 Business Days depending on the mobile money network. SwiftPay's processing fee on the original transaction is non-refundable in all cases.

8.3 Chargebacks & Reversals

A chargeback or forced reversal may occur where a Payer or mobile money network operator disputes a transaction. In the event of a chargeback:

  • SwiftPay will notify the Merchant within 2 Business Days of receiving the chargeback notification.
  • The Merchant has 5 Business Days to provide evidence contesting the chargeback, including transaction records, delivery confirmations, or communication logs.
  • If no evidence is provided, or SwiftPay determines the chargeback is valid, the disputed amount will be debited from the Merchant's account.
  • A chargeback administration fee may be applied per disputed transaction, as communicated in the Dashboard fee schedule.

8.4 Fraud-Related Disputes

Where a dispute involves suspected fraud by the Merchant or their systems, SwiftPay reserves the right to immediately freeze the Merchant's account and withhold settlement funds pending investigation. SwiftPay may cooperate with mobile money operators, financial regulators, and law enforcement in such investigations.

Merchants with a consistently high dispute or chargeback rate may be subject to enhanced monitoring, increased settlement hold periods, or account suspension. SwiftPay recommends Merchants implement robust fraud prevention checks on their own platforms.
