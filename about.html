NexaPay is a mobile money payment gateway operated by Elicate Technologies Limited, registered in the Republic of Zambia. We provide a single, unified API that lets developers and merchants accept mobile money payments across Southern Africa — without dealing with individual telecom integrations, complex compliance requirements, or fragmented settlement systems.
01
Our Story

NexaPay was born out of a simple frustration: collecting mobile money payments in Southern Africa was unnecessarily hard. Developers had to integrate separately with each telecom operator — MTN, Airtel, Zamtel, EcoCash — each with its own API, documentation, and approval process. Merchants were left with fragmented settlement reports and no single view of their money.

Elicate Technologies Limited was founded in Ndola, Zambia, with a mission to remove that friction. We spent months building direct integrations with every major mobile money operator in the region, creating a robust middleware layer, and designing an API that any developer could understand and deploy in hours — not weeks.

Today, NexaPay powers payments for e-commerce stores, logistics platforms, microfinance institutions, schools, and freelancers across Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Namibia. We're just getting started.

NexaPay does not use third-party payment aggregators. We interface directly with licensed mobile money network operators in each country, giving us greater reliability, lower latency, and direct settlement relationships.

Mission & Vision

Our mission is to make mobile money payments as simple as sending an email — for any developer, anywhere in Southern Africa.

Our vision is a Southern Africa where digital commerce is accessible to every business, no matter how small, and every payment settles reliably, transparently, and instantly.

We believe that payment infrastructure should be invisible — it should just work. Merchants should be able to focus on building their products, not debugging telecom API errors at 2 AM. Developers should be able to go live in an afternoon, not a quarter.

Everything we build — from our API design to our dashboard UX to our support response times — is guided by the question: "Does this make it easier to collect a payment in Southern Africa?"

Our Technology

NexaPay is built on a modern, cloud-native stack designed for the reliability demands of financial infrastructure. Our architecture priorities:

  • Direct operator integrations — we connect to each mobile money operator's production API directly, eliminating aggregator latency and single points of failure.
  • Idempotent payment processing — every API call can be safely retried without risk of double-charging, using unique idempotency keys.
  • Real-time webhooks — payment status updates are delivered to merchant endpoints within seconds of the operator confirming the transaction.
  • Multi-region infrastructure — our servers are distributed across multiple data centres in Southern Africa to minimise latency and ensure high availability.
  • End-to-end encryption — all data in transit is encrypted with TLS 1.3. All stored credentials and sensitive data are encrypted with AES-256.
  • Automated reconciliation — our settlement engine automatically reconciles transaction records with operator reports, flagging any discrepancies before they affect merchants.
