Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about fees

Everything you need to know about how NexaPay charges for transactions.

How is the transaction fee calculated?
The fee is percentage of the transaction amount + flat fee. For example, a ZMW 250 Zambia payin would cost: (ZMW 250 × 2.6%) + ZMW 0.50 = ZMW 6.50 + ZMW 0.50 = ZMW 7.00 total fee. The merchant receives ZMW 243.00. You can choose to pass the fee to the customer or absorb it yourself — this is configurable per payment link or API call.
Are there any setup or monthly fees?
No. NexaPay has no setup fees, no monthly fees, no minimum transaction requirements, and no account maintenance fees. You only pay when a successful transaction is processed. Failed transactions are not charged.
When do I receive my settlement?
Standard settlement is T+1 business day for Zambia, Botswana, and Namibia (T+2 for Zimbabwe). Settlements are processed in local currency to your registered bank account. Enterprise merchants can negotiate same-day or custom settlement schedules.
What happens if a transaction fails?
Failed transactions are not charged. You only pay a fee when a transaction successfully completes and the funds are received from the mobile money operator. Timeout, insufficient funds, and network rejection events are all free.
Are volume discounts applied automatically?
Yes. Volume tier discounts are calculated and applied automatically at the start of each billing month, based on your total successful transaction count in the previous 30 days. You do not need to apply or request a discount — it is applied to all transactions in the current month.
Is there a charge for refunds or disputes?
Refunds are free to issue. The original transaction fee is not refunded when a merchant initiates a refund (as the payment was successfully processed). Dispute investigations are handled at no charge. NexaPay does not levy chargeback fees — mobile money networks rarely support chargebacks in the traditional sense.
Do rates include VAT?
Rates shown on this page are exclusive of VAT. VAT applies in Zambia (16%), Botswana (14%), Namibia (15%), and Zimbabwe (15%) where NexaPay's services constitute a taxable supply. VAT-registered merchants can claim input tax credits. NexaPay will issue a tax invoice for all fees charged.
