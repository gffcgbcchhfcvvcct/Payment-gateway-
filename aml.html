IMPORTANT COMPLIANCE NOTICE. This Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy governs all use of NexaPay's payment gateway, APIs, and financial services. NexaPay operates across Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Namibia and is fully committed to compliance with the applicable AML laws and regulations in each jurisdiction. All customers, employees, and partners are bound by this policy.

Introduction
Our commitment to clean finance

NexaPay (a payment gateway platform operated by Elicate Technologies Limited) is committed to preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illegal activities through our financial services across Southern Africa.

This Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy outlines the measures we take to comply with applicable laws and regulations in all four jurisdictions where we operate, including:

  • Zambia — the Prohibition and Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PPML Act) and Financial Intelligence Centre Act
  • Zimbabwe — the Money Laundering and Proceeds of Crime Act (MLPCA) and Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe guidelines
  • Botswana — the Financial Intelligence Act and the Proceeds of Serious Crime Act
  • Namibia — the Financial Intelligence Act (FIA) and Bank of Namibia AML/CFT directives

We also adhere to international AML standards set by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) and the Eastern and Southern Africa Anti-Money Laundering Group (ESAAMLG).

NexaPay's payment infrastructure spans mobile money networks across Zambia (MTN, Airtel), Zimbabwe (EcoCash, Innbucks), Botswana (Orange Money, Mascom MyZaka), and Namibia (MTC MobiPay, Bank Windhoek). This cross-border reach makes robust AML controls essential to every transaction we process.

Objectives
What this policy sets out to achieve
Prevention

Stop NexaPay's platform from being used for money laundering, terrorist financing, or any other form of financial crime across Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Namibia.

Compliance

Ensure full compliance with all applicable AML laws, regulations, and financial intelligence directives in every jurisdiction where NexaPay operates.

Risk Management

Identify, assess, and mitigate risks associated with money laundering and terrorist financing across all customer segments and transaction types.

Reporting

Establish clear procedures for detecting and reporting suspicious activities to the relevant financial intelligence units in each country we operate.

Scope
Who and what this policy covers

This AML Policy applies to all customers, employees, contractors, and third-party service providers involved in the use of NexaPay's services across our four operating markets. It covers:

  • Mobile money payment processing (MTN, Airtel, EcoCash, Innbucks, Orange Money, Mascom MyZaka, MTC MobiPay)
  • Payment API users and integrated merchants
  • Virtual card holders and digital wallet users
  • Cross-border payment and settlement users
  • Businesses using NexaPay's merchant dashboard and payment links

Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
Know Your Customer — verified and risk-assessed

NexaPay implements comprehensive Customer Due Diligence (CDD) measures across all four jurisdictions to verify customer identities and assess the risk associated with their activities. Our KYC process includes:

Identity Verification

We collect and verify government-issued identification from all customers during onboarding:

  • National ID card, passport, or driver's licence
  • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement — not older than 3 months)
  • Business registration documents for merchant accounts
  • Tax Identification Numbers (TIN) where applicable per jurisdiction

Risk Classification

All customers are classified into one of three risk tiers based on their profile, transaction history, geographic exposure, and business nature:

Low Risk — Streamlined onboarding, standard monitoring Medium Risk — Additional verification, periodic review High Risk — Enhanced Due Diligence required, senior approval

Ongoing Transaction Monitoring

NexaPay continuously monitors customer transactions for patterns consistent with money laundering, structuring, layering, or terrorist financing. Our systems apply automated rules and threshold alerts calibrated for each country's regulatory requirements and local transaction norms.

NexaPay will not onboard or continue to serve any customer who refuses to provide required identification documents or who provides false information. Accounts will be immediately suspended pending investigation.

Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
Elevated scrutiny for high-risk relationships

For customers classified as high-risk, NexaPay applies Enhanced Due Diligence measures that go beyond standard CDD. EDD is triggered by factors including — but not limited to — Politically Exposed Person (PEP) status, operations in high-risk jurisdictions, complex ownership structures, or unusual transaction volumes.

EDD measures include:

  • Obtaining detailed information on the customer's source of funds and source of wealth
  • Conducting enhanced background checks and adverse media screening
  • More frequent transaction reviews (monthly or as warranted)
  • Requiring senior management approval before onboarding or continued engagement
  • Applying lower transaction thresholds and tighter velocity controls
  • Obtaining additional documentation on the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship

Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs): Customers who are PEPs — or immediate family members and close associates of PEPs — are automatically subject to EDD regardless of their transaction volumes. This applies to PEPs from all four of NexaPay's operating countries and internationally.

Suspicious Activity Reporting
Detecting and escalating red flags

NexaPay is legally obligated to report suspicious activities to the relevant financial intelligence authorities in Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Namibia. All staff are trained to identify and escalate the following red flags:

  • Unusual or unusually large transactions with no apparent legitimate business purpose
  • Transactions structured in patterns that appear designed to avoid reporting thresholds (structuring / smurfing)
  • Transactions involving counterparties in jurisdictions identified as high-risk by FATF
  • Customers reluctant to provide required KYC documentation or who provide inconsistent information
  • Sudden unexplained increases in transaction volume or value inconsistent with the customer's profile
  • Rapid movement of funds through accounts with no clear commercial rationale
  • Attempts to evade record-keeping, reporting requirements, or compliance checks
  • Payments to or from sanctioned individuals, entities, or jurisdictions

Tipping-off is prohibited. NexaPay employees are strictly prohibited from informing any customer or third party that a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) or Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) has been filed or is under consideration. Violation of this prohibition may constitute a criminal offence under the laws of each jurisdiction.

Record Keeping
Secure retention of compliance records

NexaPay maintains comprehensive records to support regulatory oversight, legal proceedings, and internal compliance reviews. All records are stored securely with strict access controls and tamper-evident audit logging.

What We Retain

  • All customer identification documents and KYC records
  • Transaction records — including amounts, parties, timestamps, and channels
  • Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) and Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs)
  • Correspondence with regulatory authorities
  • Internal compliance decisions, risk assessments, and audit trails

Retention Period

Records are retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of the transaction or the end of the customer relationship — whichever is later — in compliance with the laws of Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Namibia. Where local law requires a longer period, the longer period applies.

Upon lawful request, NexaPay will promptly provide records to the Financial Intelligence Centre (Zambia/Namibia), Financial Intelligence Unit (Zimbabwe), or Financial Intelligence Agency (Botswana) in the format required by each authority.

Employee Training
Building a compliance-first culture

All NexaPay employees — regardless of role — receive mandatory AML training as part of their onboarding and on a continuing basis. Employees in compliance, operations, and customer-facing roles receive additional role-specific training.

Our training programme covers:

  • The AML laws and regulations applicable in Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Namibia
  • How to recognise money laundering typologies and red flags
  • Internal escalation and suspicious activity reporting procedures
  • Customer Due Diligence and Enhanced Due Diligence requirements
  • Record-keeping obligations and data protection standards
  • Sanctions screening and PEP identification procedures
  • Tipping-off prohibitions and legal obligations

Training is reviewed and updated at least annually, or whenever there is a material change in regulations or NexaPay's product offering. Completion of mandatory training is tracked and forms part of each employee's compliance record.

Third-Party Relationships
Holding partners to our standards

NexaPay conducts formal due diligence on all third-party service providers — including mobile money operators, banking partners, payment processors, and technology vendors — before entering into any business relationship with them.

Our third-party AML requirements include:

  • Verification that the third party holds all required licences and regulatory approvals in their jurisdiction
  • Assessment of the third party's own AML/KYC programme and controls
  • Contractual obligations requiring ongoing AML compliance
  • Periodic re-assessment of third-party risk profiles
  • Right to audit or request evidence of AML compliance from high-risk third parties

NexaPay will immediately terminate relationships with any third party found to be knowingly facilitating money laundering, terrorist financing, or sanctions evasion, and will report such findings to the relevant authorities.

Internal Controls & Audits
Continuous improvement of our AML programme

NexaPay maintains robust internal controls to ensure ongoing compliance with this AML Policy and the applicable laws across all four markets.

Our internal control framework includes:

  • A dedicated Compliance Officer responsible for AML programme oversight
  • Automated transaction monitoring systems with configurable rule sets per jurisdiction
  • Segregation of duties between compliance, operations, and product teams
  • Regular internal audits of AML procedures — at least annually
  • Independent external audits conducted as required by regulatory authorities
  • Board-level reporting on AML programme effectiveness and key risk indicators
  • Formal escalation procedures for compliance breaches or near-misses

Findings from audits are documented, tracked to resolution, and used to improve our AML programme. Material findings are reported to senior management and relevant regulatory bodies where required.

Policy Review
Keeping pace with evolving regulations

This AML Policy is a living document. It is reviewed and updated at least annually and immediately upon any of the following triggers:

  • Material changes in AML legislation in Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, or Namibia
  • Updated FATF guidance or ESAAMLG regional typologies affecting Southern Africa
  • Significant changes in NexaPay's products, services, or geographic operations
  • Findings from internal or external audits that require policy updates
  • Identification of new money laundering or terrorist financing typologies in our markets

All updates to this policy are communicated to relevant employees and stakeholders and are made effective from the date published. The policy version is recorded together with the effective date.
